10 National Parks You Must Visit This Year

Priya Anand
National Parks Editor
July 10, 2026 6 min read

Ask ten travelers for their favorite national park and you'll get ten different answers — that's the point. Between the 63 designated parks in the system, there's a version of America for every kind of traveler: canyon country, alpine lakes, desert badlands, temperate rainforest. After several seasons chasing permits and campsites, here are the ten that consistently deliver.

The headliners, and why they still hold up

Grand Canyon, Yellowstone and Yosemite get the crowds for a reason — the scale of each is genuinely hard to process in photos. The trick with all three is timing: arrive at opening time, or plan your big viewpoints for the last two hours before sunset when the tour buses have thinned out.

The quieter picks

For something with more solitude, look at North Cascades in Washington (fewer visitors than Yosemite gets in a single weekend), the Everglades for an entirely different, water-based park experience, and Great Basin in Nevada, home to some of the darkest skies left in the country.

  • Grand Canyon, AZ: Rim trails are free and stunning; the permit-only inner canyon hikes need booking months out.
  • Yellowstone, WY: Go in shoulder season (late May or September) for thinner crowds and active wildlife.
  • Zion, UT: The shuttle-only main canyon means arriving early is the only way to beat the lines.
  • Glacier, MT: Going-to-the-Sun Road is only fully open a few months a year — check conditions before you plan around it.
  • Acadia, ME: The only park on this list where a car and a lobster roll get you most of the highlights.
The parks people remember most aren't always the biggest ones — they're the ones where you had room to actually stop and look.

Permits and timing

The parks with timed-entry systems (Zion, Rocky Mountain, Arches, Glacier) sell out their reservation windows weeks in advance during peak season, so build your permit search into the very first step of planning, not the last. For the rest, arriving before 9am solves most crowd problems on its own.
