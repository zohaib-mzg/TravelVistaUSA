The Ultimate Road Trip Guide to the Pacific Coast

Sarah Johnson
Travel Expert
July 15, 2026 8 min read

There's a reason the Pacific Coast Highway shows up on every "drive before you die" list — and having done it three times now, I can say the reputation is earned. This isn't a highlight reel route where you rush between famous overlooks. It's a slow, winding drive where the best moments happen between the stops: a fog bank rolling over a headland, a diner with a two-item menu, a beach nobody else has found yet.

Planning your route

Most people drive the stretch from San Francisco to San Diego, but if you have the time, start further north in Mendocino. The northern section is quieter, the redwoods are close enough to touch the road, and you'll avoid the worst of the summer traffic around Big Sur. Budget at least five days if you want to actually get out of the car — three is enough to drive it, not enough to experience it.

Where to stop

Big Sur gets the postcards, and it deserves them, but don't skip Cambria, a low-key town with tide pools and a genuinely good bakery. Further south, the Santa Barbara stretch trades cliffs for palm-lined boulevards — a nice change of pace before you hit the traffic of Los Angeles.

  • Mendocino: Redwood groves minutes from the water, and far fewer crowds than Big Sur.
  • Bixby Bridge: The single most photographed stretch of the route — arrive at sunrise to have it to yourself.
  • Cambria: A quiet, walkable base with tide pools and elephant seals just up the road.
  • Santa Barbara: A good place to slow down for a night before the final push south.
The drive rewards patience more than speed — the best views are the ones you weren't expecting.

Practical details

Fill your tank whenever you see a station; long stretches between Big Sur and San Simeon have none. Cell service disappears in places, so download offline maps before you go. And if you're driving in winter or early spring, check for road closures — landslides periodically take out sections of Highway 1, sometimes for months at a time.

Whatever time of year you go, build in slack. The best day of our last trip wasn't on the itinerary at all — it was an afternoon we spent parked at a pullout south of Big Sur, waiting for the fog to clear, watching whales spout offshore. That's the drive working the way it's supposed to.
