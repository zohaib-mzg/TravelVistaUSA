Best Weekend Getaways in the USA

Diego Alvarez
City Guides Editor
July 6, 2026 7 min read

Not every trip needs a week off work. Some of the best travel memories I have came from a Friday-night flight and a Sunday-evening return — you just have to pick the right city and skip the stuff that eats time without adding much.

Chicago: architecture, deep dish, one river cruise

Chicago rewards a tight weekend better than almost any US city. Base yourself downtown, walk the Riverwalk, and book the architecture river cruise for Saturday morning before the crowds build. Save one dinner for deep dish, but don't make it every meal — the neighborhood spots outside the Loop are where the city actually shows up.

Charleston: history without the school-trip feel

Charleston's historic district is genuinely walkable in a weekend, and the food scene has outgrown its "Southern charm" reputation into something worth the trip on its own. Skip the horse-carriage tour and instead book a small-group walking tour — you'll get better stories and move at your own pace.

  • Savannah, GA: Spanish moss, squares you can wander for hours, and some of the best brunch in the South.
  • Asheville, NC: Mountain views, a genuinely great beer scene, and easy access to the Blue Ridge Parkway.
  • Portland, ME: Small enough to see on foot, with a food scene that punches well above its size.
A good weekend trip isn't a compressed vacation — it's a different kind of trip, built around fewer, better decisions.

Making it actually feel like a break

The mistake most people make with short trips is over-scheduling them. Pick two "must-do" items per day and leave real gaps — the best weekend memories are usually the unplanned ones anyway.
